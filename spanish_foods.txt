pozole - spicy mexican soup with pork, radish, cabbage
cochinita pibil - yucatecan pork in achiote and citrus, slow-roasted in banana leaves
chile relleno - cheese-stuffed poblano, egg-battered and fried, often in tomato salsa
sincronizadas - two flour tortillas with ham and melted cheese, griddled flat like a pressed quesadilla
enchiladas - corn tortillas dipped in salsa, rolled around filling, baked with sauce and cheese on top
quesadilla rellena - thick folded or stuffed quesadilla packed with cheese plus meat, mushrooms, or other fillings
mole poblano - rich dark sauce from puebla with chiles, seeds, spices, and a hint of chocolate, over poultry
enchiladas verdes - enchiladas sauced with tart green tomatillo-serrano salsa, often chicken, crema, and cheese
tacos de birria - tacos of slow-braised spiced meat (often beef or goat) with consomé for dipping
carnitas - pork shoulder simmered until tender then crisped in its own fat, shredded for tacos with onion and cilantro
dragonzito - tiny paper pouches of sweet-sour-chile powder, classic candy eaten straight or dusted on fruit and cucumbers from street carts
tamales - masa spread with filling, wrapped in corn husks or banana leaves, and steamed until the dough turns fluffy and pulls from the husk
pambazo - soft bun dipped in guajillo salsa, seared on the plancha, and stuffed with papas con chorizo, lettuce, crema, and queso
queso fundido - skillet-baked melted cheese, often with chorizo or roasted rajas, scraped onto hot tortillas right from the sizzle
empanadas - crimped pastry pockets, fried or baked, filled with anything from picadillo and queso to pumpkin or cajeta depending on the region
chile verde - meat braised until tender in a chunky green gravy of tomatillos, green chiles, garlic, and cumin, scooped with rice or tortillas
flan - egg custard set in a mold lined with dark caramel, chilled, then flipped so the syrup coats every glossy slice
enchiladas de mole - tortillas softened in rich mole, rolled or folded around chicken or cheese, topped with toasted sesame, crema, and crumbly queso
arroz con leche - short-grain rice cooked slowly in milk with sugar, cinnamon, and a little vanilla until thick and spoonable, warm or cold
